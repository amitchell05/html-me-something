About Me

I am a front-end developer student located in Florissant, MO. I hope to become a front-end developer for companies that value diversity, intristic and altruistic values, and their users.

In 2018, I completed LaunchCode’s LC101 program, where I learned valuable, in-demand web development skills. I used Python and Java to build websites that utilized a relational database to create, sort, edit, and remove information. Also, I learned how to create static and dynamic websites using HTML5, CSS3, and Bootstrap. The front-end topics covered in LC101 strongly drew my interest because of my academic background in the arts.

I earned a Bachelor of Liberal Studies degree from the University of Missouri-Saint Louis, where I studied art and music performance. After graduating from LC101, I dedicated my time to studying digital painting, web design, HTML5, CSS5, and JavaScript online at my own pace. The online course helped me understand front-end concepts, but I wanted to learn how to build user-friendly websites. In August, I was accepted into LaunchCode’s CoderGirl Front End development track to learn JavaScript, Angular, responsive web design, and other concepts to help me become a front-end web developer.

Currently, we are halfway through Unit 1, which covers JavaScript, TypeScript, and Angular. The next units will cover responsive web design and career readiness. I am excited to learn more about these topics and expect to graduate in April 2021.

I am determined to develop a career that allows me to be authentic, creative, and detail-oriented.
